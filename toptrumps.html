TOP TRUMPS


Prime Minister Theresa May

Vs.

President of the European Commission Jean-Claude Juncker



A leading advocate of deeper EU integration, Juncker was Luxembourg’s Prime Minister for 18 years, making him one of the longest-serving democratically elected leaders in the world but his downfall came with a scandal involving widespread bugging of the security services. He has also been described by the Dutch finance minister as a “heavy smoker and drinker”.

Likely winner: Juncker – unlike May he has managed to unify all EU leaders behind him and is determined to avoid other states following Britain’s lead of leaving the block. He stated after a meeting with Theresa May in May 2017 that “I leave Downing Street 10 times more sceptical than I was before” and May’s own advisors have been asking “If she cannot lead, why should she expect anyone to follow?”
